O’Hare, after shredding myself apart

Autumn doesn’t last forever.
The sun eventually sets earlier than it should
and the trees become bare in their endeavor
to shred themselves apart for another year as you’d
watch and expect it, even, nodding your head: This is right. The sun is setting now.
I am at that estuary of autumn & winter, day & night.
It’s dusk, wading in the crook of a brow—
feeling the edge of the twilight.

Sometimes I dream about nights with the sun caught in a haze
like a fire throbbing beneath its smoke.
Those dreams are always the strangest, when the glaze
of hope colors the gray with a glow like yolk.
My teeth ache in front of lavender shoes,
and hiding beneath the caramel & chocolate yarn is a green & red bruise.

Bleeds Into Mine

Biking down the street, his expression bleeds into mine.
His eyes are sharp—drill a depression that bleeds into mine.

Faintly humming to deathly crimson, glowed eyes like wine
Skin picks apart—thoughts turn to digression—bleeds into mine.

Light to dark, like a blackout—it must not be fine.
Some kind of spiraling obsession bleeds into mine.

Dark to light, blinking rapidly—it must be a sign.
Some kind of impulsive delusion/decision bleeds into mine.

I walk like a wood table with a jagged scarred line.
Would you ask me how this suppression bleeds into mine?

Tracing a path along my mood, waving like a sine
graphs a distance—watch as the skin/question bleeds (into mine).

Near Millennium. septum pierced. flirting? please define
being (feeling?) liked before this illusion bleeds into mine.

Can’t play sports because I guess I’m crooked like my spine.
Scoliosis, trans. Their aggression/oppression bleeds into mine,

waters me down as I seek to make myself align
with worn/torn fantasy: chest compression bleeds into mine.

You would expect with a name of light that I would shine
but instead—the opposite impression bleeds into mine.
			
Mustard Yellow

I rise from the pillow, the case a skin mustard yellow.
I must’ve been dreaming in mustard yellow.

I was a toddler when Baba first kicked Mamá
when I learned to meander in mustard yellow.

New York is lined with food carts: a hot dog with
ketchup and a not-so-thin mustard yellow.

Lavender, honey, plum, canary (fly), I-
ris(ked) blond midnight butterscotched within mustard yellow.

I know I haven’t been drinking enough water 
because the rivers of pee grin mustard yellow.

The same old vers -es -us, discordant.
“She/her,” he said (in) mustard yellow.

He supports me, but I’m not a boy in his eyes, lips
or brain. So where am I? Chagrin, mustard yellow.

“Mummy,” as I wrote in kindergarten, cooks
rice, potatoes, leaves of mustard, yellow

turmeric in the air and the walls sticky
with the tattoos of what’s been: mustard yellow.

Needle: stitch, guacamole: green, water: clear
morning: why did I wake up, Google: Sergey Brin, mustard: yellow

clipped wings, thick sigh, laughter, Baba’s smile, whisper
(“)I love you,(”) after what I’ve seen—what a sin—mustard yellow.

Shame on you: you’ve just now realized that all
of your dreams are coated in mustard yellow.
			
learned to love...

I’ve abandoned my calculator in my desk drawer
like logic. I always come back home with sticky flan fingers dipped in loyalty,
and love, laughter in the foreground. Background: chai, chamomile tea, coffee.
I step foot into Queens, inhale tacos and garbage, avoid suspicious puddles, and crash into her.

Like in that physics midterm, I’ve come to recognize doom as a burning murmur
in the back room…I was made to love you. You became my whole personality.
“I saw you when you were born.” Rip my legs apart. Hand him a dead girl I could never be.
Sometimes shrimp cocktail flies on the floor before a slammed door

and I (July 28, 2022)—the thought that has the loudest voice is “kill yourself.”
…Everything would be better without me—looked at the window (sixth floor), wished I could
But all there was…a loud bang as I drove my fist into a metal bystander.
The door echoed its dull moan, came apart to flatline lips, furrowed brows, his voice on a shelf.
He can do that while I wish everything was over and would he cry or just wield a fork if I would?

But all there is: cling to cigarette smoke-stained smiles even if I can’t stand (how you treat) her.
			
trying to make sense of it

But all there is: cling to cigarette smoke-stained smiles even if I can’t stand (how you treat) her.
Your deep laugh and scratchy blue-tinted stubble now dotted with white rings in my ears
like a sunset, a Jarritos ending too soon, another
midnight bleeding into 3 AM, or clinging to the fantasy of New Year’s
even if the magic of staying up late and watching the ball drop has long faded.
The way you used pick me up and carry me before you went to work
and I’d look forward to being too high to remember what I hated.
Looking down at the floor, I’d forget the broken plates and get lost in a euphoric murk.

I focus on the lights: give me distance
as my mom wipes her eyes and nose with toilet paper, her bruises
shades and hues of viole(n)t that blare “feel my existence.”

The few times that this happens, my mouth refuses
to speak.						I’m relieved when you start to laugh again. 
You’re feeding your tetras or cooking a sauce for jhol momos, and I’m grasping for the mundane.

your lullaby from hyde park to harvey, minty fresh against my ear.
			
if I could see myself through your eyes

to [redacted]

Lake Michigan lies between our two lying land masses—
a body of water between two bodies of flesh & bone & too many feelings all at once.
Water waves & sound waves coalesce across miles:
		If you could see yourself through my eyes…

I have a hard time letting what you say ripple into me.
So, I take it and fold it into an origami butterfly. 
At least I can avoid looking at it while admiring its beauty.
The tone of your voice—it must have some meaning for it to throb within my (rib)cage. 
There’s always someone trying to reach me and make me live past thirty, 
but their voices always feel so distant. So many paper animals scattered across my desk.

I trace the folds and wonder whether I should open it.
It’s taken so long for me to unravel this wire of childhood trauma around my insides,
and insecurity wrapped around my neck, that kissable neck as you called it, and I reach you,
and your sleepy smile as I tiptoe across that highwire. 

		Look at me.

I can’t. I can’t watch you look at me because all I see reflected in your piercing eyes
is what has been brewing inside of me for years, what is folded in my desk drawer:
		It starts off, in some dull blue ballpoint ink on college-ruled looseleaf,
				THINGS I HATE ABOUT MYSELF
		When I wrote it, I was desperate and I wanted to remind myself, remind everyone,
		why they couldn’t love me.

		Lower your arms.

I look up with the most reluctant, pained expression on my face.
Your gaze traces my fingertips and that gentle voice settles into the pit of my stomach,
not like stones this time but like a fleet of actual butterflies, 
fluttering as your eyelashes do sometimes.
		This time it’s not unrequited, I tell myself, wanting so badly to believe it.
You guide me through the unfolding and slowly everything is laid out and stilled: 
how you want to teach me to look into your eyes and see past the reflection.

And part of me thinks it’s bullshit.
It’s only been a bit less than two weeks.
It took three weeks of texting and a chai meetup for Brandon to reject me…
and then ghost me after I asked to be friends. That part hurt the most,
because I like to say that losing a friend is worse than losing a lover. 
But I was never really mad at him. Maybe he just didn’t know what to say. 
Because I never do. I couldn’t even really look at him at Chiya Chai that day—
what made me think that I(t) was tolerable (looking at me, I’m so fucking ugly)?
I guess I was naive enough to think that I didn’t fuck everything up on that day it drizzled
and some guy harassed me afterward in the men’s restroom of Millenium Station.
And I think, 
		Sometimes things get better before they get worse, 
		and the star twinkles one last time before it explodes.

You feel like a ghost sometimes, an apparition that I made up in my mind to cope.
Sometimes when I’m walking and the biting wind hits me, I ask if you’re real.

It’s a question that, to a normal person, would have a strictly defined answer.
Cogito, ergo sum. I think, therefore I am. Some Descartes shit like that.
To me, all my memories feel blurred and you start to feel like a Schrödinger’s cat,
simultaneously both alive and dead, real and fake,
a thought experiment that shows the absurdity and paradoxical nature of my mind:
		I need to open the box to know.
		But, Pandora’s box? Will the cat die from my eyes? Will misery be unleashed?

Are you real? 
It’s a question that has no answer for me, but I’m willing to open the box to find out.
Call me Pandora, tell me that curiosity kills the cat. But sometimes, this is all you can do.
And this is all I know:

		When we’re staring at each other through the glass screen, 
		your eyelashes make a mess out of me—
		tangle me into something I can’t define.
		And that’s when I recognize that you are real
		because I can see the mirror breaking.

		The cracks ripple across the surface like constellations or creases all laid out
		and the stars keep twinkling and I keep laughing, all giddy & stupid & 
		stuck in the way you call me love.
			
Projection

I sit and watch the late-night cartoons in your eyes,
flickering across the television when my dad sits to eat dinner 
after coming home from work at
2 am.

Like him, you walk in, the door clicking open so
easily because you have the key
and I let you in like I’m supposed to and
suddenly, I’m above the ground in your arms
before you leave again.
We kissed before you hopped on the Greyhound,
right on the lips.
I didn’t want you to go
so I sat on my bed and moped after.

The deadlock is broken: I know it’s worn from (t)rust.
And I wore you (down), draped across my torso like a vest
as I ran my hands through your hair in the middle of the night,
letting you forget your nightmares.

And you ran your hands through mine,
ready to rid me of my fears.

Memories burst forth from the projector, flickering across my eyes,
even as I try to cover them.
This fascist attempt at censorship never works.
Yet, you are not here to watch them with me
		and I am not sure you ever will be again.
